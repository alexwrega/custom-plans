Skill Gap Analysis

Ranked by frequency of errors across 8 Grade 3 STAAR reading assessments (avg score 73.1%, 74 total wrong answers). Inference and text evidence are the two largest gaps, each driven by consistent misreads of “what can the reader conclude” and “which detail best supports” question types.

  • 1
    Inference & Drawing Conclusions
    Misses “what can the reader conclude,” “what can the reader infer,” and diagram-completion questions across stories, informational text, and poems. Especially common in questions about character motivation and relationships.
    16 errors
  • 2
    Text Evidence & Supporting Details
    Picks the wrong sentence when asked which detail “best explains,” “best supports,” or “best shows.” Misses both explicit retrieval and evidence-for-a-claim questions across literary and informational passages.
    13 errors
  • 3
    Text Features & Graphics
    Trouble with cause→effect diagrams, photograph captions, subheadings, and section titles. Often selects a text feature that exists but answers a different question than what is being asked.
    11 errors
  • 4
    Summary
    Picks summaries that focus on a single detail or leave out the resolution instead of accurately capturing the whole story or passage. Particularly consistent across the 2013 and 2014 tests.
    7 errors
  • 5
    Author’s Purpose & Craft
    Misses why an author includes specific imagery, an opinion, or a claim — and why a poet uses punctuation such as exclamation points. Confuses the descriptive “what” with the purposeful “why.”
    6 errors
  • 6
    Vocabulary & Context Clues
    Misses antonyms, synonyms, and dictionary-entry meaning questions. Includes one unanswered item. Generally improving — only 1–2 errors per test on most assessments, but the 2013 test reveals a more serious gap.
    6 errors
  • 7
    Theme, Main Idea & Central Message
    Difficulty identifying the central idea of an informational article or the lesson/main message of a story. Picks a supporting detail instead of the overarching message.
    5 errors
  • 8
    Poetry Elements & Craft
    Misses questions about stanza structure, rhyme scheme, and what specific lines contribute to meaning. Also one error on identifying narrative poetry by its story-telling quality.
    4 errors
  • 9
    Plot, Drama & Poetry Structure
    Confuses story problem/resolution (why a character is upset, what leads to the solution) and the role of specific plot events in moving the story forward.
    3 errors
  • 10
    Cross-Text Comparison & Synthesis
    Two errors on paired-text questions asking how two selections “are alike” and how they “are different” on the same topic.
    2 errors
  • 11
    Text Structure
    One error on how information in a recipe section differs structurally from the surrounding selection.
    1 error
