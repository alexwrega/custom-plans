Skill Gap Analysis

Ranked by error frequency across all 11 Grade 4 reading assessments. Emma is a generally solid reader (most scores in the 70s–80s) whose errors cluster around higher-order comprehension — especially inference, vocabulary in context, and author’s purpose. She is reliably accurate on literal recall, but struggles when she has to read between the lines.

  • 1
    Inference / Drawing Conclusions
    Top gap by a wide margin. Misses character-motivation questions, “what is probably true” questions, and what an author implies but doesn’t state.

  • 2

    Misses meanings of grade-level words from surrounding context: distinct, lure, absurd, peculiar, irritating, trough, qualified, precedes, appropriate.
    11 errors
  • 3
    Author’s Purpose
    Misses why an author included a paragraph, photo, or section. Trouble identifying intent (entertain vs inform vs persuade) and tone toward a subject.
    10 errors

    Compare & Contrast
    Cross-text questions (“one way the two are alike/different”) and within-text comparisons consistently trip her up.
    9 errors

    9 errors

    7 errors
