Skill Gap Analysis

Ranked by frequency of errors across 9 Grade 7 reading assessments (avg score 81.1%, 70 total wrong answers). The 2019 test (14 wrong, 66.7%) was the lowest performance. Inference is by far the most persistent gap, followed closely by Author’s Purpose and Summary.

  • 1
    Inference & Drawing Conclusions
    Misses what imagery “suggests,” what a reader can “infer,” and what a sentence or line conveys about a character’s motivation — across stories, poems, dramas, and informational texts.
    17 errors
  • 2
    Author’s Purpose & Craft / Rhetoric
    Misidentifies why an author includes an example, a description, a quotation, or sensory language; confuses persuasive purpose with argument type; mistakes factual claims for opinions.
    13 errors
  • 3
    Summary & Central Idea
    Picks summaries that focus on one detail instead of capturing the whole selection; picks a theme that is too specific or off-center from the author’s message.
    10 errors
  • 4
    Cross-Text Comparison & Synthesis
    Struggles with questions asking what two selections “both emphasize,” what one text does differently, or which action appears in a poem but not in a paired article.
    9 errors
  • 5
    Figurative Language & Word Choice
    Misreads what personification “describes,” what repetition “emphasizes,” what imagery “creates a sense of,” and what irony signals in a play scene.
    9 errors
  • 6
    Vocabulary & Context Clues
    Missed vocabulary items asking for the meaning of omission, refuted, waver, illustrious, and chain (dictionary-entry format).
    6 errors
  • 7
    Text Structure & Organization
    Confuses how an author organizes information — missing problem-solution vs. chronological distinctions, and what an organizational pattern “emphasizes.”
    4 errors
  • 8
    Character & Narrative Analysis
    Misidentified character motivation (why a letter matters, how a narrator is characterized) and missed how setting influences plot.
    4 errors

MobyMax Remediation Plan Summary

Focused 5-part plan: 52 highest-priority MobyMax Grade 7 lessons capped at 284 XP. Inference comes first because it underlies every other skill type; Author’s Purpose and Summary follow since Lily’s 2019 collapse (14 wrong) was driven heavily by those two gaps. Figurative Language and Character work close out the plan.

Part 1: Inference & Text Evidence
8 lessons 56 XP
  • Make inferences (informational and literary)
  • Cite textual evidence to support an inference or claim
  • Choose the strongest vs. weakest evidence
  • Distinguish explicit from implicit information
Part 2: Author’s Purpose, Craft & Rhetoric
11 lessons 50 XP
  • Determine author’s purpose and point of view
  • Rhetorical questions and irony
  • Evaluate arguments: claims, reasoning, evidence validity
  • Analyze text organizational structures
  • Stage directions in drama
Part 3: Summary, Central Idea & Theme
10 lessons 70 XP
  • Determine central idea by analyzing paragraph main ideas
  • Identify and write objective summaries
  • Differentiate key ideas from supporting details
  • Identify stated and unstated theme in literary texts
  • Recognize how character, conflict, and setting develop theme
Part 4: Figurative Language, Cross-Text & Vocabulary
12 lessons 55 XP
  • Identify and interpret similes, metaphors, personification
  • Connotation and how word choice creates mood
  • Author bias and multiple interpretations
  • Compare and contrast themes across forms and genres
Part 5: Character, Narrative & Poetry Structure
11 lessons 53 XP
  • Flat/round, static/dynamic, protagonist/antagonist characters
  • How dialogue, thoughts, and actions reveal point of view
  • Analyze how characters affect plot and vice versa
  • Poetry structure: stanzas, lines, rhyme, lyric vs. narrative
  • Drama: theme development
